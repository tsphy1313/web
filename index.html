Topu Saha

Introduction

Topu Saha is a PhD candidate in Applied Physics, working in the field of Applied Optics and Remote Sensing to monitor atmospheric insects. He, along with other members of his research group, developed an autonomous Near-Infrared optical sensor system to detect insect activities remotely, both in the laboratory and field conditions. His expertise includes evaluating the performance of different optical sensors, prototype designing for remote sensing, integration of hardware and software for data acquisition, analyzing optical signals to retrieve different features, application of machine learning techniques for classification, and statistical analysis of the dataset to study different morphological properties and population dynamics of flying insects.

Mr. Saha is highly dedicated to conducting impactful and innovative research, his aim is to explore various fields of advanced remote sensing technology to contribute to a more sustainable and resilient future for mankind.

Research

*Click image for details


Projects:

  • Multi-sensor calibration to investigate the flight pattern of insects

    - Integrated camera and a single-pixel photodetector to simultaneously record data from a flying insect
    - Simulated insect flight pattern at different camera viewing angles
    - Predicted flight orientation of an insect from optical signals

  • Development of a fully-autonomous solar powered optical sensor system

    - Developed a compact, portable, and easily field-deployable optical sensor system
    - Designed a solar circuit to power sensing and control components for automated field operation
    - Implemented a real-time thresholding algorithm to detect insect flight signals
    - Implemented secure data transfer using SSH protocol for remote communication

  • UAV propeller characterization using frequency analysis of Lidar signals

    - Simulated LiDAR backscatter signals from drone rotor blades
    - Conducted controlled experiment to validate optical backscatter characteristics under real flight conditions
    - Applied FFT to analyze frequency components of LiDAR signals distorted by drone blade motion

  • Monitoring mosquito eco-systems and vector-control strategies using a stand-off optical sensor

    - Developed a stand-off optical sensor for continuous, non-invasive monitoring of flying insects
    - Validated multiple sensors to identify the most durable and optimal configuration for field deployment
    - Conducted over 25 months of field experiments to monitor insect activity patterns across seasons
    - Extracted different optical signatures of an insect and analyzed their response to varying environmental factors
    - Applied different ML models to classify and cluster insect species based on optical sensor data
